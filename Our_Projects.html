Shree Ashok Singhal Rugn Seva Sadan

Published on : 14th June - 10:33

A Home away from home for poor cancer patients coming into Mumbai for treatment.


The biggest challenge for poor patients and their families in a city like Mumbai is finding accommodation, which is an additional burden as they are already in a difficult situation with medical expenses.

We strive to provide clean and hygienic accommodation, food & transportation to Tata Cancer Hospital for poor Cancer patients and their families coming to Mumbai for treatment.
Some of the facilities we provide are, Dometories & individual rooms to stay, full-fledged in-house kitchen, and 24/7 Ambulance service for transportation to the hospital, Satsang Hall for Yoga & daily Prarthana.
At present, we have a facility for 100 patients and 2 care-take per patient, total of 300 people stays at any given point in time.
All the facilities are free of cost for the patients and a nominal cost ischarged for the relatives.


Chatrapathi Shivaji Maharaj Dialysis Centre

Published on : 14th June - 11:52

Chronic Renal Failure is a condition where a patient needs hemodialysis depending upon the severity of his/her condition. This condition is becoming alarmingly common worldwide. Hemodialysis is a costly affair and even more difficult for a patient from a lower-income group.

At our Charitable Dialysis Centre, we are working towards achieving top-quality Hemodialysis facilities at the most affordable cost to poor patients.

1. 9 Dialysis machines with state-of-the-art support equipment.
2. Separate isolated facility for Seropositive patients.
3. We work in 3 shifts to serve 24 patients daily (about 600 dialyses monthly)
4. Our team comprises very experienced nephrologists, RMOS, Well Trained Dialysis Technicians & Paramedical staff, Nursing staff, and a housekeeping team to maintain cleanliness at all times.
5. An ambulance facility is available at all times.
6. Our services are extended free of cost to patients coming from low socio-economic backgrounds.
7. We have implemented an HMS system for efficient and smooth operations for data capturing, maintaining medical protocols, scheduling, etc...

About Us

Shiv Kalyan Kendra, as an NGO was established in 1986 by Vishwa Hindu Parishad (Kokan Pranth) at Hanuman Tekdi, Sion Koliwada, Mumbai.
